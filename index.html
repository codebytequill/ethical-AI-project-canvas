Ethical AI Project Canvas

A guided tool to assess the ethical considerations of an AI project.

1. Problem & Impact

Is the problem you're solving a real, validated user need?

Why this matters

Ethical AI starts with solving real problems, not creating solutions in search of one. Unvalidated problems can lead to useless or even harmful products.

Have you considered the potential for negative dual-use or unintended consequences?

Why this matters

A technology designed for good can often be repurposed for harm. Considering these "dual-use" scenarios early is a critical part of responsible innovation.

2. Data Collection & Privacy

Is your training data representative of your user population to avoid bias?

Why this matters

If your data doesn't reflect the diversity of your users, your model will likely underperform for and discriminate against underrepresented groups.

Is all personally identifiable information (PII) handled securely and with user consent?

Why this matters

Data privacy is a fundamental user right. Mishandling PII can lead to severe legal, financial, and reputational damage, and harms your users directly.

3. Model Fairness & Transparency

Do you have a clear plan to test for and mitigate algorithmic bias?

Why this matters

Fairness doesn't happen by accident. It requires deliberate testing (e.g., slicing data by demographic groups) and mitigation strategies to ensure equitable outcomes.

Can you explain the model's decisions to a non-technical user?

Why this matters

Explainability builds trust. If users (or internal teams) don't understand why an AI makes a certain decision, they can't trust it, debug it, or appeal it.
